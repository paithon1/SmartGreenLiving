Home Solutions


Here are seven practical and easy home solutions to make your home greener and cleaner, benefitting both the environment and tour quality of life. Whether you're a seasoned environmental enthusiast or new to sustainable living, this page offers great ideas to transform your home into a more enjoyable space for you and the planet.


1. Reduce, Reuse, Recycle


Embracing the "reduce, reuse, and recycle" mantra in your daily life helps cut down on waste and conserve valuable resources, leading to a more eco-friendly and sustainable home. By minimizing single-use items, repurposing goods, and participating in recycling programs, you contribute to a greener future while reducing your ecological footprint.

Learn more


2. Buying Energy-Efficient Appliances


Investing in energy-efficient appliances for your home not only lowers your utility bills but also reduces carbon emissions, making your household more eco-friendly. By choosing appliances with high Energy Star ratings, you can enjoy the benefits of cutting-edge technology while minimizing your environmental impact. Energy-efficient appliances typically cost $30-200 more than uncertified appliances. However, when you subtract the rebates and energy savings, it becomes a much more appealing option.

3. Composting


Composting is a controlled, aerobic (oxygen-required) process that converts organic materials into a nutrient-rich soil amendment or mulch through natural decomposition. By turning our food scraps and yard trim into compost, we can transform our waste streams into a beneficial, value-added soil amendment and use it to protect the environment and create resilient communities.

4. Reduce Single Use Plastic


Reducing single-use plastic in your home is a sustainable choie that minimizes waste and contributes to a cleaner environment. By using reusable alternatives like cloth bags and stainless steel containers, you can help create a greener and more eco-friendly living space. Also, reducing single-use plastic can lead to cost savings in the long run.



5. Start a Garden


Starting a garden at home is a fulfilling and eco-friendly initiative that not only adds natural beauty to your surroundings but also promotes sustainability by reducing your carbon footprint. Additionally, it provides a great source of fresh, organic produce right in your backyard, contributing to a greener and healthier lifestyle.



6. Wash Your Laundry in Cold Water Instead of Hot Water

Washing your laundry in cold water is an eco-conscious choice that conserves energy and reduces your utility bills while cleaning your clothes just as well as hot water.



7. Make Use of Natural Light as Much as Possible

Maximizing the use of natural light in your home is an energy-efficient choice that reduces electricity consumption in your home. Additionally, it also creates a brighter, natural, and more inviting living space.
